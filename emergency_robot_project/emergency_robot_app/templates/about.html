About Emergency Robot Management System (ERMS)

The "Earthquake Emergency Response Robots" project aims to create, develop, and implement systems specifically designed to handle post-earthquake situations. The main focus of the project is to build adaptable robots that come equipped with sensors and communication capabilities.
These robots will be able to navigate through areas affected by disasters, assess the extent of damage, locate survivors, and deliver essential supplies. By incorporating cutting-edge technologies, like artificial intelligence and remote operation interfaces, the robots' autonomy and effectiveness in responding to changing and unpredictable situations are greatly enhanced.
The ultimate objective is to enhance rescue operations efficiency, safety measures, and response speed, thereby contributing towards disaster management and mitigation efforts.

Disclaimer: This project is still in the testing and prototype phase.
